My Responses:

1. The internet works by breaking information down into smaller packets of
information being passed from one server to another to then pass the information
on to the computer.

2. IP address is the exact address or location of your computer. This is how
information is able to be distinguished between computers and other locations.
Thanks to IP addresses, information is able to be delivered to the correct
computer. DNS translates IP addresses from numbers to words, making it easier to
understand. DNS was originally created for government and educational use.

3. A cookie is a small piece of information stored on the user's computer from
the website.

4. The three parts of an URL are:
   1. Protocol
   2. Domain Name
   3. Path
