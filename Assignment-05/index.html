Simple Old Fashioned Chocolate Cookies

as found in Fannie Farmer's Cookbook

Walking through your grandma's door, a wave of love hits you in the face.
What is that smell?
Exploring the house you discover your grandma has made homemade cookies.
Have you ever noticed that no matter how you try, your cookies don't smell or even taste the same?
I had this problem, until I found my grandma's secret recipe book!
Not even my mom had the right recipe!
My grandma used a book called Fannie Farmer's Cookbook. We never knew that Grandma was using the cookie recipes from this book. Using this recipe, you will be able to mimic and relive those wonderful memories.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 egg or 2 egg yolks, well beaten
  • 1 tablespoon cream or milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/2 cup cocoa powder

Directions

  1. Cream butter
  2. Beat in sugar, egg, creamormilk and vanilla
  3. Add flour, salt, cocoa powder and baking powder sifted together
  4. Mix well
  5. Arrange by teaspoonfuls on buttered cookie sheet, 1 inch apart.
  6. Bake about 8 minutes at 375 degrees. Makes 50-60
  7. To emphasize the butter flavor, brush the cookies lightly with melted butter while they are still warm
