How to Complete a Beginner Counted Cross Stitch: From Store to Frame


The objective of these instructions is to teach students of WebDev how to complete a simple counted cross stitch. By following these steps, they will gain simple techniques that can take a person without these instructions years of experimenting to discover. By the end of this tutorial the students will be able to complete a counted cross stitch. When completing a counted cross stitch, you will make mistakes. This is common of even experienced stitchers. As time goes on you will learn to improvise or just decide it is easier to back up and correct the mistake. Happy stitching!


Skill Level:

Beginner (no prior experience needed)


Storage:

Store in a clean dry place.


Supplies Needed:

  • 5x7 counted cross stitch kit (make sure it is not stamped)
  • 6” embroidery hoop
  • Needle threader


Key Terms:

  • Canvas- material used for counted cross stitch
  • Counted cross stitch- a craft that requires the user to count squares on a blank canvas to complete a pattern.
  • Cross stitch: a stitch used that looks like an “x”
  • Symbols: marks that identify which color to use on a pattern.


Table of Contents:

  2. Organizing Supplies
  3. Creating a Cross Stitch
  4. Ending the Thread

Select work location

  • Pick a place that is well lit.


Organizing Supplies

  1. Fold Canvas in quarters-create a crease.
  2. Place hoop on canvas with center in the middle
  3. Note: Canvas needs to be tight in the hoop. This makes it easier to work with.
  4. Look at the pattern:
    • Notice there are four arrows that point to the center of the pattern.
    • Use a light highlighter and trace lines in the middle.
    • Note: This will divide the pattern into sections.


Creating a Cross Stitch

  1. Match the symbol in the center with its thread color.
  2. Pull apart the thread
    • Note: For a clean start only pull apart one thread (The instructions say 2 for a cross stitch), this will allow for your back to look cleaner.
  3. Fold thread in half.
  4. Use needle threader.
  5. Place needle threader through the eye of the needle.
  6. Place thread through eye of the needle threader.
  7. Pull the needle threader out of the eye of needle.
  8. Pull needle from the back of the canvas at the center square.
    • Note: Do not pull all the way through yet, with one hand hold the loop.
  9. Pull needle back through the hole diagonal of the start point in the square.
    • Note: This is where you are going to pull the needle through the loop.




  10. Pull needle through the hole right next to where needle just pulled through.
  11. Pull the needle through the diagonal hole.
    • Note: This will complete the “x” for the cross stitch.




Ending the Thread

  1. Pull the thread through to the back.
  2. Run the thread under the stitches in the back.
  3. Make a knot
  4. Cut thread


Notes:

  • Continue Steps 3 and 4 for each color
  • Remember this is called counted cross stitch, you will need to count your spaces as you go.
  • Pay close attention to your pattern it is easy to get your count off.
  • Do not work too far ahead with one color, this will make it easier to make a mistake.
  • If you make a mistake just back track and correct it.
  • It may get frustrating when you make a mistake, just take a deep breath and remember you are still learning.
  • Your pattern may have other stitches, refer to the instructions for simple look at each stitch, another good resource is youtube.com.
